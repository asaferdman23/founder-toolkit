## Due Diligence Prep

### Documents to Have Ready
- [ ] Pitch deck (12-15 slides, PDF)
- [ ] One-pager / executive summary
- [ ] Financial model (Google Sheets, 3-year projection)
- [ ] Product demo video (2-3 min Loom)
- [ ] Customer interview summaries (Maya Dan, Lihi quotes)
- [ ] Cap table (current ownership)
- [ ] Technical architecture diagram
- [ ] Competitive landscape matrix

### Common DD Questions

**Market:**
- How big is the event production market in Israel? → $2B+, ~20 major production companies
- Why hasn't someone done this before? → Kneret Productions tried (pure marketplace, failed on quality). We solve quality with AI + vetting + producer backup.
- What's the expansion path? → Israel → English-speaking markets → Europe

**Product:**
- How does AI replace a producer? → It doesn't fully — it handles 80% (planning, vendor matching, budget, timeline). Producer backup for the 20%.
- What's the tech moat? → Proprietary supplier matching algorithm + vetted network + event-specific AI models trained on Israeli market
- How do you handle supplier quality? → Deep categorization, onboarding vetting, portfolio review, customer ratings, "ProduceGerX Verified" badge

**Business:**
- What's churn look like? → Events are recurring (2-4/year). Once you've set up vendors, switching cost is high. Target: <10% annual churn.
- How do you acquire customers? → LinkedIn outreach to HR managers, event industry events, referral program (event planners talk), content marketing
- What happens if producers lower prices? → Our value isn't just price — it's transparency, one platform, AI planning, and time savings (1.5-3 months → 1 week)

**Team:**
- Why you? → [Founder's event industry experience / tech background / domain expertise]
- Who else do you need? → Head of Sales (HR relationships), 1-2 more engineers
- What's the hiring plan? → 3 people Year 1, grow to 8 by Year 2

## Investor Meeting Checklist

### Before Meeting
- [ ] Research the fund's portfolio (any event/HR/marketplace investments?)
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions FOR the investor
- [ ] Have demo ready (app.producegerx.com with demo data)
- [ ] Print one-pager if in-person
- [ ] Know your numbers cold (TAM, CAC, LTV, burn rate)

### During Meeting
- [ ] Lead with the 40% markup hook
- [ ] Show product (don't just talk about it)
- [ ] Share Maya Dan quote as social proof
- [ ] Be honest about risks (supplier quality, marketplace cold-start)
- [ ] Ask: "What would you need to see to move forward?"

### After Meeting (within 24 hours)
- [ ] Send thank-you + deck + any requested materials
- [ ] Log meeting notes in CRM
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder (1 week)
- [ ] Update other investors on momentum ("just met with X")
